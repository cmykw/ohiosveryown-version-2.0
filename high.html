High Museum of Art.

Contributions: User Interface, User Experience,
Information Architecture, Iconography.

Overview

The High Museum of Art is the leading art museum in the Southeastern U.S. with more than 14,000 works of art in its permanent collection. I was approached to design a ticketing navigation/purchase path that would be completely touch responsive for mobile kiosks.

Problem: Before the High implemented ticket purchasing kiosks, there was no automated way for patrons to buy tickets at the museum. When the museum held special events, this would often lead to long lines for patrons, and on occasion the events selling out without a forewarning to unknowing patrons.

Solution: I wanted to first and foremost increase productivity for the High by creating a great user experience. This was possible by designing a perspicacious user interface and congruent, harmonious information architecture flow. In turn, the kiosks will help patron flow by creating a simple, easy to use way to purchase and reserve tickets.
